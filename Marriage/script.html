The wedding party gathers.

The MC says:
For generations,
we have used words and rings
to create the sacred covenant of marriage.
_______ and _______
you are about to speak the words
and exchange the rings
that will make formally unite you in marriage.

The partners say:
Be my covenanted partner, in love and friendship, in peace and companionship, in the eyes of God and humanity.

They exchange rings inscribed with אני לדודי ודודי לי.

The partners recite:
We promise.
To protect.
To serve.
To learn.
To grow.
We promise.
That your life shall be mine.
And yours shall be mine.
We promise.
To build ourselves and eachother.
To live and let live.
To meet in the middle.
We promise.
To talk.
To discuss.
To learn.
We promise.

The couple says:

May there be good fortune and good luck for us and all Israel!

The festivities begin.

The ritual is intentionally short. I don’t see myself having a big wedding, I think I would be very bored. To me, the wedding would be the least significant part of a relationship. I think that I can and will have a strong committed relationship without the assistance of a large wedding ceremony. I want my wedding to be me and my friends and my significant other’s friends having fun, and to achieve that I have cut a lot of things.

The core of the ceremony is the poem I wrote – it’s very much inspired by John Adam Ross’ assertion that ritual is performance. It serves a very similar goal to the ketubah, and for that matter I might have it inscribed on something to be hung in our home, but unlike the ketubah, which is written and formal, it would be living, performed in front of an audience.

I also really loved the marriage vows from the same sex marriage document, and I love the passage אני לדודי ודודי לי, from the song of songs. I want that to be inscribed on the inside of my ring to remind me and my partner that we are in a covenant.

I would imagine that the ceremony would be surrounded by celebration, but that would be primarily unscripted. I also composed a playlist of songs that I would like to played at my wedding, which I think do a great job of describing how I feel well done relationships work.
