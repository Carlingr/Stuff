It took some digging to find it, but I really love this chapter. It really encapsulates the carnality of amorous relationships. I love how unrestrained it is in it’s descriptions, but I also really love how, while it has a significant amount of breast imagery, it also spends a non insignificant amount of time talking about the subject’s body as a whole. In some ways it seems less sexualizing than some modern texts, because it speaks about loving someone in their entirety.
