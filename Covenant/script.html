Script

The community sings:

. הִנֵּה מַה טוֹב וּמַה נָּעִים שֶׁבֶת אָחִים גַּם יַחַד


During the singing the baby is brought into the room. The person carrying the baby should be cloaked, as they are to symbolize the lifeforce that brought this being into the world.

The baby is handed to the parents.

The community says

ברוך הבא


The parents say:

Every person born into this world

represents something new,

something that never existed before,

something original and unique.

It is up to us know and recognize

that this new person is unique in the world,

a singular character' and personality,

and that there has never been anyone like this child before -

for if there had been,

there would have been no need for this new life to be in the world.

We hope that our child may find their place among our community.

We hope that this community will find its place around our child.


The community says:

We accept amongst ourselves the responsibility of raising this child. They will not learn only from their parents, but also from each one of us – as it is said “It takes a village to raise a child.”


The parents hang a jewish star around their child’s neck.


The parents turn to the chair of Elijah and say:

We bring this child into the world and into the covenant before the great teacher of the Covenant, Eliyahu Hanavi. We hope that as Eliyahu protected the covenant our child too will find reason to protect humanity.

Each new birth is testimony to our conviction

that we still believe that life is good,

that life is worthwhile.


The community says:

Hope is here.

Hope is our celebration,


The parents say:

Let us give that hope a new name.



The parents say the prayer for making first decisions about gender for a child.

Hashem, whose mysterious nature patterns our own, this day we prayerfully call this child our child. Let the gender choices we make as we name our child be for the good. Let us seek to learn the truth about this child's gender from this child over time, and let us not force a gender-identity upon this child, based on these eight brief days of our acquaintanceship. Hashem, we have long awaited this child's arrival in our lives. Let the power of our love and anticipation guide us to teach and nurture our children in humility, to guide and steer them with strength and beneficent authority, and to hold in awareness, that though their naked selves are entrusted to our care, much of their truth has yet to be revealed.

Barukh atah Adonai creator and knower of mysteries.


The parents announce the name of the child, and the reason they picked the name.


The community welcomes the new child by saying their name, followed by

We welcome you to our world and our community, may you find your place among us, filling those gaps which we still seek to fill, and may you share your knowledge and wonder with those outside their community. May you never feel stifled by our expectations, may you always feel supported by our hopes.


The parents respond

Amen.

The ritual is really mostly about community. If I circumcise my child I will mostly be doing it because I want them to be able to be a part of the Jewish community. That said, I would also never want them to feel stifled by the community they were born into. The ritual tries to balance the need of a community to raise a child, and my hope that my child will find places where they feel at home, as I have found jewish places where I feel at home, with my hope that my child is happy above all, even if it means making decisions other than those which I might have hoped for them.

The ritual is also intentionally constructed to be gender neutral. I strongly believe in a post-gender society. Gender is not a concept that really makes sense to me, and I would never want to force any gender on my child. While I understand that I will make some decisions about gender-identity for my child, I would want them to feel comfortable taking on any and all gender roles they feel comfortable in, and I want to recognize that in what is historically a strongly gendered ritual.
