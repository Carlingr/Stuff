I’ve gotta be honest. Right now, I’m not feeling this whole “Brit Milah” thing. It’s not so much that I have anything against it, as much as it is that I’m apathetic. That said, there are certainly interesting concepts in Judaism concerning entering the covenant, So here are some concepts that I’d like you to approach this ritual thinking about.

  1. A physical symbol of Judaism. Should you have a son, being circumcised would help to create a strong Jewish identity for him. Right now, I’m not creating a ritual that centers around circumcision. I want you to make that decision with your significant other. This ritual is centered around the concept of a physical symbol concerning Judaism, but one that, if your child decides later in life, they can remove, and one that will apply to your child, no matter their sex.

  2. Brit milah, or simchat bat are both strongly gendered rituals. As someone who is currently very thankful for their gender neutral name, I hope that you will choose a name that fits your child, no matter what gender they will be. Whatever the ceremony is, I hope that you will understand both the irrevocable role gender plays in our society (or at least my society, it’d be great if gender wasn’t a thing in your society), and that gender is a construct, and can change.

  3. Community. The punishment for not being circumcised is caret, which is generally understood to mean being exiled from the Jewish community. If you have a son, being circumcised will be a large part of his identity. Not being circumcised would make him significantly different from his Jewish peers.

  4. Biblical Commandment. Lord knows I don’t much care about biblical punishment right now, but perhaps you will. Anywho, know that Avraham was commanded to circumcise at 90, did so, and circumcised all of his children. Know that the punishment for not being circumcised is caret, which is generally understood to mean being exiled from the jewish community. Know that moses was nearly killed because he did not circumcise his son. To me, these are not good reasons to make a decision, but perhaps they will be for you.
